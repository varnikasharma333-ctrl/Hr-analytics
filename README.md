HR Analytics Dashboard
📊 Project Overview
This project features an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard designed to help organizations understand employee attrition patterns. By analyzing various demographic and professional factors, the dashboard provides actionable insights to improve employee retention and workplace satisfaction.

🚀 Key Features
KPI Tracking: Real-time visibility into Total Employee Count, Attrition Count, Attrition Rate, and Average Age.

Demographic Analysis: Breakdown of employee distribution and attrition by Age Group and Gender.

Education & Department Insights: Visualizations showing how attrition varies across different education fields (Life Sciences, Medical, Marketing, etc.) and departments (R&D, Sales, HR).

Job Satisfaction Map: A detailed matrix (heat map) correlating Job Roles with satisfaction scores to identify "at-risk" positions.

Interactive Filtering: A dynamic "Education" slicer allows users to filter the entire dashboard by degree level (Bachelor's, Master's, Doctoral, etc.).

📈 Dashboard Highlights
The dashboard answers critical business questions, such as:

Who is leaving? (Attrition Rate by Gender and Age)

Where is the turnover highest? (Department-wise and Education-wise Attrition)

Are employees happy? (Job Satisfaction Rating by Role)

Data Visualization Breakdown:
Donut Charts: Department-wise Attrition.

Bar Charts: Employee Count by Age Group and Education Field.

Area/Line Charts: Attrition Rate by Gender across different age brackets.

Matrix/Heatmap: Job Satisfaction ratings across various organizational roles.

💡 Insights Derived
The R&D Department represents the largest portion of the workforce but also a significant volume of attrition.

Employees in the 26-35 age range show the highest frequency of turnover.

Job Satisfaction is notably lower in specific roles like Sales Representatives, suggesting a need for policy review in those sectors.
